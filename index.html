004 — Projects

Engineering work.

01
Reciprocating Saw Mechanism Design
SolidWorks · FEA
200 N Cutting Force Full BOM + Drawings Fatigue Safety Validated
◎ Objective

Design a cam-follower mechanism capable of delivering a 200 N cutting force for a reciprocating saw application, progressing from concept through release-ready documentation.

◈ Methodology

Conducted detailed feasibility analysis and determined load profiles and motion requirements. Built complete 3D models in SolidWorks, produced drawings and BOMs. Performed FEA to evaluate stress, deformation, and fatigue safety factors across all critical components prior to fabrication.

◉ Results

Mechanism successfully delivers 200 N cutting force within design constraints. FEA confirmed adequate fatigue safety factors across all load-bearing components. Complete release-ready documentation package produced including drawings, BOMs, and revision records.

◇ Future Improvements

Add dynamic simulation to analyze vibration behavior under cyclic loading. Explore lightweight material substitutions to reduce overall mechanism weight while maintaining force output.

02
PS5 Controller Joystick Holder — DFM
SolidWorks · 3D Printing
DFM Principles Prototype Tested Production Drawings
◎ Objective

Design a functional consumer product component — a PS5 controller joystick holder — applying DFM principles to ensure the design is optimized for low-cost fabrication and repeatable manufacturing.

◈ Methodology

Modeled the component in SolidWorks with DFM constraints in mind. 3D printed a prototype sample and performed hands-on fit and function testing. Iterated on geometry based on test feedback until all product specifications were met.

◉ Results

Final design meets all fit and function specifications. Production drawings completed with DFM-optimized geometry, minimizing material waste and machining complexity. Component validated through physical prototype testing.

◇ Future Improvements

Evaluate injection molding feasibility as a higher-volume manufacturing path. Conduct drop/impact testing to validate durability under real-world use conditions.

03
Ladder Chassis Frame — Composite Structural Design
SolidWorks · Abaqus
34.7% Weight Reduction Multi-Layup FEA Equivalent Strength
◎ Objective

Design a full ladder chassis structural frame using fiberglass composite laminates that achieves significant weight reduction compared to a steel baseline while maintaining equivalent structural strength under all critical load cases.

◈ Methodology

Evaluated material options and selected layup orientations based on load path analysis and cost requirements. Modeled the full frame in SolidWorks and performed static FEA in Abaqus across multiple load cases. Iterated geometry and layup schedule to optimize strength-to-weight ratio.

◉ Results

Achieved a 34.7% weight reduction compared to the steel baseline with structurally equivalent performance confirmed across all FEA load cases. Complete fabrication drawings and material specifications produced for manufacturing handoff.

◇ Future Improvements

Extend analysis to dynamic and fatigue loading scenarios. Explore carbon fiber hybrid layups for further weight savings. Validate FEA predictions against physical coupon testing.

04
Scroll Compressor Kinematic & Thermodynamic Simulation
MATLAB · SolidWorks
1,000+ Line Model Publication Track P-V Curve Algorithm
◎ Objective

Develop a first-principles computational model of a scroll compressor that accurately characterizes compression cycle behavior — including working chamber volumes, fluid pocket dynamics, and thermodynamic state changes — to support design optimization and research publication.

◈ Methodology

Built a 1,000+ line MATLAB model integrating kinematic geometry, thermodynamic equations, and computational geometry. Engineered a fluid pocket tracking algorithm to compute dynamic P-V curves, discharge pressures, and volumetric efficiency as a function of crank angle.

◉ Results

Model successfully computes dynamic P-V curves, discharge pressures, and volumetric efficiency across the full compression cycle. Fluid pocket tracking algorithm accurately captures gas pocket formation and evolution. Research progressing toward publication under faculty supervision.

◇ Future Improvements

Incorporate heat transfer and leakage loss models for higher fidelity simulation. Extend the model to parametric design optimization. Validate against experimental compressor test data prior to publication.
